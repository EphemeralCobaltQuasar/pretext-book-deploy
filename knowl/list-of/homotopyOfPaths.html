Definition 1.2.39. homotopy of paths.

A homotopy of paths in \(X\) is a family \(f_t : I \to X\text{,}\) \(0 \le t \le 1\text{,}\) such that:
ðŸ”—
  1. The endpoints \(f_t(0) = x_0\) and \(f_t(1) = x_1\) are independent of \(t\text{.}\)
    ðŸ”—
  2. The associated map \(F : I \times I \to X\) defined by \(F(s,t) = f_t(s)\) is continuous.

ðŸ”—
When two paths \(f_0\) and \(f_1\) are connected in this way by a homotopy \(f_t\text{,}\) they are said to be homotopic. The notation for this is \(f_0 \simeq f_1\text{.}\)
