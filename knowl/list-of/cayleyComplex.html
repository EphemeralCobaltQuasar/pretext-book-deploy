Definition 1.2.74. cayley complex.

Covering spaces can be used to describe a very classical method for viewing groups geometrically as graphs. Recall from Corollary 1.28 how we associated to each group presentation \(G = \langle g_{\alpha} \mid r_{\beta} \rangle\) a 2-dimensional cell complex \(X_{G}\) with \(\pi_{1}(X_{G}) \approx G\) by taking a wedge-sum of circles, one for each generator \(g_{\alpha}\text{,}\) and then attaching a 2-cell for each relator \(r_{\beta}\text{.}\) We can construct a cell complex \(\tilde{X}_{G}\) with a covering space action of \(G\) such that \(\tilde{X}_{G} / G = X_{G}\) in the following way. Let the vertices of \(\tilde{X}_{G}\) be the elements of \(G\) themselves. Then, at each vertex \(g \in G\text{,}\) insert an edge joining \(g\) to \(g g_{\alpha}\) for each of the chosen generators \(g_{\alpha}\text{.}\) The resulting graph is known as the Cayley graph of \(G\) with respect to the generators \(g_{\alpha}\text{.}\) This graph is connected since every element of \(G\) is a product of \(g_{\alpha}\)â€™s, so there is a path in the graph joining each vertex to the identity vertex \(e\text{.}\) Each relation \(r_{\beta}\) determines a loop in the graph, starting at any vertex \(g\text{,}\) and we attach a 2-cell for each such loop. The resulting cell complex \(\tilde{X}_{G}\) is the Cayley complex of \(G\text{.}\) The group \(G\) acts on \(\tilde{X}_{G}\) by multiplication on the left. Thus, an element \(g \in G\) sends a vertex \(g'\) to the vertex \(g g'\text{,}\) and the edge from \(g'\) to \(g' g_{\alpha}\) is sent to the edge from \(g g'\) to \(g g' g_{\alpha}\text{.}\) The action extends to 2-cells in the obvious way. This is clearly a covering space action, and the orbit space is just \(X_{G}\text{.}\)
