Definition 1.2.69. orbit space.

Given an action of a group \(G\) on a space \(Y\text{,}\) we can form a space \(Y / G\text{,}\) the quotient space of \(Y\) in which each point \(y\) is identified with all its images \(g(y)\) as \(g\) ranges over \(G\text{.}\) The points of \(Y / G\) are thus the orbits \(G y = \{ g(y) \mid g \in G \}\) in \(Y\text{,}\) and \(Y / G\) is called the orbit space of the action. For example, for a normal covering space \(\tilde{X} \to X\text{,}\) the orbit space \(\tilde{X} / G(\tilde{X})\) is just \(X\text{.}\)
