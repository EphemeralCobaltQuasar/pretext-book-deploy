Definition 1.2.24. complex projective n space.

Example 0.6. Complex projective n-space \(\mathbb{C}P^n\) is the space of complex lines through the origin in \(\mathbb{C}^{n+1}\text{,}\) that is, 1-dimensional vector subspaces of \(\mathbb{C}^{n+1}\text{.}\) As in the case of \(\mathbb{R}P^n\text{,}\) each line is determined by a nonzero vector in \(\mathbb{C}^{n+1}\text{,}\) unique up to scalar multiplication, and \(\mathbb{C}P^n\) is topologized as the quotient space of \(\mathbb{C}^{n+1} - \{0\}\) under the equivalence relation \(v \sim \lambda v\) for \(\lambda \neq 0\text{.}\)
