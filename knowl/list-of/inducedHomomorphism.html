Definition 1.2.53. induced homomorphism.

Suppose \(\varphi : X \to Y\) is a map taking the basepoint \(x_0 \in X\) to the basepoint \(y_0 \in Y\text{.}\) For brevity we write \(\varphi : (X, x_0) \to (Y, y_0)\) in this situation. Then \(\varphi\) induces a homomorphism \(\varphi_* : \pi_1(X, x_0) \to \pi_1(Y, y_0)\text{,}\) defined by composing loops \(f : I \to X\) based at \(x_0\) with \(\varphi\text{,}\) that is, \(\varphi_*[f] = [\varphi f]\text{.}\) This induced map \(\varphi_*\) is well-defined since a homotopy \(f_t\) of loops based at \(x_0\) yields a composed homotopy \(\varphi f_t\) of loops based at \(y_0\text{,}\) so \(\varphi_*[f_0] = [\varphi f_0] = [\varphi f_1] = \varphi_*[f_1]\text{.}\) Furthermore, \(\varphi_*\) is a homomorphism since \(\varphi(f \cdot g) = (\varphi f) \cdot (\varphi g)\text{,}\) both functions having the value \(\varphi f(2s)\) for \(0 \le s \le \frac{1}{2}\) and the value \(\varphi g(2s - 1)\) for \(\frac{1}{2} \le s \le 1\text{.}\)
