Definition 1.2.75. subgraph.

A subgraph of a graph \(X\) is a subspace \(Y \subset X\) that is a union of vertices and edges of \(X\text{,}\) such that \(e_{\alpha} \subset Y\) implies \(\overline{e}_{\alpha} \subset Y\text{.}\) The latter condition just says that \(Y\) is a closed subspace of \(X\text{.}\) A tree is a contractible graph. By a tree in a graph \(X\) we mean a subgraph that is a tree. We call a tree in \(X\) maximal if it contains all the vertices of \(X\text{.}\) This is equivalent to the more obvious meaning of maximality, as we will see below.
