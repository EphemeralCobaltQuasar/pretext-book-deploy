A deformation retraction of a space \(X\) onto a subspace \(A\) is a family of maps \(f_t : X \to X\text{,}\) \(t \in I\text{,}\) such that \(f_0 = \mathbb{1}\) (identity map), \(f_1(X) = A\text{,}\) and \(f_t|_A = \mathbb{1}\) for all \(t\text{.}\) The family \(f_t\) should be continuous in the sense that the associated map \(X \times I \to X\text{,}\) \((x, t) \mapsto f_t(x)\text{,}\) is continuous.
