Definition 1.2.54. free product.

As a set, the free product \(\ast_{\alpha} G_{\alpha}\) consists of all words \(g_{1} g_{2} \cdots g_{m}\) of arbitrary finite length \(m \ge 0\text{,}\) where each letter \(g_{i}\) belongs to a group \(G_{\alpha_{i}}\) and is not the identity element of \(G_{\alpha_{i}}\text{,}\) and adjacent letters \(g_{i}\) and \(g_{i+1}\) belong to different groups \(G_{\alpha}\text{,}\) that is, \(\alpha_{i} \ne \alpha_{i+1}\text{.}\) Words satisfying these conditions are called reduced, the idea being that unreduced words can always be simplified to reduced words by writing adjacent letters that lie in the same \(G_{\alpha_{i}}\) as a single letter and by canceling trivial letters. The empty word is allowed, and will be the identity element of \(\ast_{\alpha} G_{\alpha}\text{.}\)
