Definition 1.2.34. smash product.

Inside a product space \(X \times Y\) there are copies of \(X\) and \(Y\text{,}\) namely \(X \times \{y_0\}\) and \(\{x_0\} \times Y\) for points \(x_0 \in X\) and \(y_0 \in Y\text{.}\) These two copies of \(X\) and \(Y\) in \(X \times Y\) intersect only at the point \((x_0, y_0)\text{,}\) so their union can be identified with the Wedge Sum \(X \vee Y\text{.}\) The smash product \(X \wedge Y\) is then defined to be the quotient \((X \times Y)/(X \vee Y)\text{.}\)
