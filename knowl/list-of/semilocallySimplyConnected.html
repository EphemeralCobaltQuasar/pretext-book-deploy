Definition 1.2.59. semilocally simply-connected.

A necessary condition for \(X\) to have a simply-connected covering space is the following: Each point \(x \in X\) has a neighborhood \(U\) such that the inclusion-induced map \(\pi_1(U, x) \to \pi_1(X, x)\) is trivial; one says \(X\) is semilocally simply-connected if this holds.
