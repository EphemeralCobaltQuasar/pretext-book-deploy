Definition 1.2.60. semi-locally simply connected.

A space \(X\) is called semi-locally simply connected if for every point \(x \in X\) and every neighborhood \(V\) of \(x\text{,}\) there exists an open neighborhood \(U\) of \(x\) with \(x \in U \subset V\) such that every loop in \(U\) can be contracted to a point within \(X\text{.}\)
