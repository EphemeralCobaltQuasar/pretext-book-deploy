    Definition 1.2.17. cell complex.

    1. Start with a discrete set \(X^0\text{,}\) whose points are regarded as 0-cells.
      ðŸ”—
    2. Inductively, form the \(n\)-skeleton \(X^n\) from \(X^{n-1}\) by attaching \(n\)-cells \(e^n_\alpha\) via maps \(\varphi_\alpha : S^{n-1} \to X^{n-1}\text{.}\) This means that \(X^n\) is the quotient space of the disjoint union \(X^{n-1} \sqcup_{\alpha} D^n_\alpha\) of \(X^{n-1}\) with a collection of \(n\)-disks \(D^n_\alpha\) under the identifications \(x \sim \varphi_\alpha(x)\) for \(x \in \partial D^n_\alpha\text{.}\) Thus as a set, \(X^n = X^{n-1} \cup_{\alpha} e^n_\alpha\) where each \(e^n_\alpha\) is an open \(n\)-disk.
      ðŸ”—

    Definition 1.2.60. isomorphism between covering spaces.

    We are interested in uniqueness up to isomorphism, where an isomorphism between covering spaces \(p_1 : \tilde{X}_1 \to X\) and \(p_2 : \tilde{X}_2 \to X\) is a homeomorphism \(f : \tilde{X}_1 \to \tilde{X}_2\) such that \(p_1 = p_2 f\text{.}\) This condition means exactly that \(f\) preserves the covering space structures, taking \(p_1^{-1}(x)\) to \(p_2^{-1}(x)\) for each \(x \in X\text{.}\) The inverse \(f^{-1}\) is then also an isomorphism, and the composition of two isomorphisms is an isomorphism, so we have an equivalence relation.

    Definition 1.2.69. cayley graph.
